My Mother's Ancestry

My mother's family history spans generations of resilience and determination. Her ancestors came from difficult backgrounds, each contributing unique cultural traditions and experiences to our family tapestry. Understanding this lineage helps me appreciate the cultural heritage I've inherited, from holiday traditions to family recipes that have been passed down through the years.

Both of my mother's parents came from Appalachia; my grandfather was from Virginia and my grandmother from Kentucky. They grew up in homes where abuse and alcoholism shattered family stability and created deep poverty. Yet in the face of these hardships, both of them developed remarkable strength and a desire to build something better for their own children.

Eventually, they each made their way to Mesa, Arizona, where they met, fell in love, and discovered the gospel of Jesus Christ. That decision changed the direction of our family forever, giving them a spiritual foundation and a new sense of hope. From those early choices came the home in which my mother, Donna, was raised and the values she later passed on to me.

Because of her parents' struggles, educational opportunities were limited, yet both of them sought training and skills that would allow them to provide for the family and move into the middle class. My grandfather’s work as a telephone company technician meant frequent moves—nineteen moves in the first twenty-one years of my mother's life. Constantly packing up and starting over left a deep impression on her and shaped her desire to create a stable, lasting home for her own children. Their story reminds me that even in difficult circumstances, courage and faith can change the course of a family.
