My Father's Ancestry

My father's side of the family offers a different, yet equally compelling, narrative. His ancestors played an important role in the development of the community of Pasco, Washington, through their work, dedication, and commitment to education. Their choices and sacrifices built the foundation on which our family stands today.

After serving as a dentist in the Army, my grandfather Kent moved his young family to Pasco to open his own dental practice. He arrived with one child, and the family soon grew to seven—six boys and one girl. My father was the third child in this busy, energetic household.

The family settled on three acres of land just outside of town. Days were filled with chores and responsibilities, balanced with school, music practice, and outdoor adventures. That property gave my father and his siblings room to explore, work hard, and use their creativity and imagination.

This love for rural living traces back to my great-grandparents, Carroll and Pearl, who lived on a dairy farm in Sunnyside, Washington. In 1941, Carroll was hired by the Morning Milk Company (later the Carnation Milk Company) to purchase milk contracts from local farmers, a job that kept him closely connected to the agricultural world.

As I look at my father's ancestry, I see patterns of hard work, faith, and dedication to both family and community. Their stories remind me that the opportunities I enjoy today were made possible by generations who were willing to sacrifice, adapt, and serve. Knowing their history helps me understand my own responsibilities and inspires me to carry their legacy forward.
